What is "If Not Us"?

If Not Us is a documentary about students fight to end school shootings, highlighting young survivors and activists turning their experiences into powerful action.

Full Synopsis

If Not Us is a compelling documentary centered on the student-led movement to end school shootings.

Highlighting the voices of those who have experienced these tragedies firsthand—alongside a generation that refuses to accept the status quo—the film explores the powerful intersection of grief and activism.

It offers an inspiring look at how young survivors and passionate advocates are finding their courage, confronting systemic inaction, and bravely answering the call for change.

Director's Statement

"In my lifetime, gun violence has sadly changed the American classroom. I was a third grader in the town over when the terrible shooting at Sandy Hook Elementary School happened. This event became a defining trauma for my generation. For too long, we have been stuck in a dangerous situation where real solutions are delayed, and meaningful change feels forever out of reach. This inaction represents a serious failure to protect the next generation. This film is dedicated to the architects of tomorrow. By centering the perspectives of the youth, those who carry the weight of the future, I aim to amplify student voices that have long been sidelined. My goal is to shift the narrative from mere conversation to the catalyst for real, systemic change. We must refuse to accept this violence as normal. The time for indifference is over. Words alone are not enough; we demand immediate and clear action to stop this crisis, and we need it now. This conversation is the essential first step."

- Patrick C
